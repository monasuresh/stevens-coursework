My Education

Lawrence Academy

Lawrence Academy is a private college preparatory school located in Groton, MA, founded in 1792. It's the 10th oldest boarding school in the US. The motto of Lawrence Academy is Omnibus Lucet which means "The light shines for all". Originally the school was called Groton Academy, but was later changed to Lawrence Academy.

My favorite class I took at Lawrence Academy was Integrated Algebra and Geometry II. In Integrated Algebra and Geometry II, I found the perfect blend of mathematical principles and their practical applications. It offered a comprehensive exploration of algebraic concepts and geometric theorems, bridging the gap between these two fundamental branches of mathematics. This integration provided a holistic perspective on mathematical problem-solving and reasoning.

One of the most memorable aspects of my time at Lawrence Academy was when I participated in a stained glass winterim. It was a very immersive experience and I ended up creating a piece that I have hanging on my wall to this day.

Bentley University

Bentley University is a private university founded in 1917 by Harry C. Bentley. It is located in Waltham, MA and was initially located in Boston's Back Bay neighborhood. The school awards bachelor of science degrees in 14 fields of business and 11 bachelor of arts degrees in fields of arts and sciences.

My favorite class I took at Bentley University was PH 254 - Special Topics in Philosophy. This his course delved into the intricate exploration of consciousness and cognition, offering a deep examination of thought-provoking concepts, including phenomenology and the continuous versus discontinuous nature of consciousness, among others.

A standout experience during my time at Bentley University was the collaborative development of a mobile app from the ground up with a team of five individuals as part of a project management course. Together, we crafted a mobile app that served as a guide to the Freedom Trail.

Stevens Institute of Technology

Stevens Institute of Technology is a private research university located in Hoboken, NJ. The university was founded in 1870 and is one of the oldest universities dedicated to technology. Initially the school was solely for mechanical engineering. The campus is 55 acres and located on Castle Point which is known to be the highest point in Hoboken. Stevens University encompasses four academic divisions that provide a range of technology focused STEM programs, as well as offerings in business, arts, humanities, and social sciences.

My favorite class from the classes that I have completed is Introduction to Database Management Systems. This course equipped me with the foundational skills to craft both basic SQL statements and intricate SQL queries. Furthermore, I gained proficiency in employing the WITH clause and delved into database views. Additionally, I delved into the intricacies of database normalization, adding depth to my understanding of this essential aspect of database management.

One of the most memorable aspects of my time at Stevens is learning how to write complex sql queries to aggregate results from multiple tables in a set-oriented way. The art of crafting complex queries laid a solid foundation for my understanding of relational databases.
